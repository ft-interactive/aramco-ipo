Saudi Arabia’s rulers wish to restructure the economy and shift it away from the dependence on crude oil. In seeking to create a more efficient state, the kingdom has decided to privatise and offer shares in its national champion Saudi Aramco, the state oil company. Assuming that investors will be offered part of Saudi Arabia huge oil reserves, Aramco should be worth a lot. It could be one of the largest listed companies in the world.

The FT has estimated Aramco’s value using a financial model, and a lot of assumptions. Its total value depends primarily on two variables: the oil price and its tax rate.

The oil price is important as we do not expect production to vary greatly over the long term.

A lower tax rate, currently at 85 per cent, would help pay for a dividend.

Investors will probably see Aramco as an income stock. Above you can change these two variables, or choose some pre-set scenarios, to see for yourself how these affect Aramco’s valuation. It has stated it plans to list 5 per cent of Aramco.
