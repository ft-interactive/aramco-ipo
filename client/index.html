Saudi Arabia’s rulers wish to restructure the economy and shift it away from the dependence on crude oil. In seeking to create a more efficient state, the kingdom has decided to privatise and offer shares in its national champion Saudi Aramco, the state oil company.

Assuming that investors will be offered access to Saudi Arabia's huge oil reserves, Aramco should be worth a lot. It could be one of the largest listed companies in the world.

The FT has estimated Aramco’s value using a financial model, and a lot of assumptions. Its total value depends primarily on two variables: the oil price and oil production.

While the oil price is important, production will matter, too. A lower tax rate, now expected to be 50%, would help pay for a dividend. Investors will probably see Aramco as an income stock, but that's a different calculation, see our article on the topic.
